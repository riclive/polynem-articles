This briefing synthesizes three converging domains — digital sovereignty, energy-to-compute transformation, and geopolitical realignment — into a unified strategic framework for Canadian leadership.

## Executive Summary

Canada possesses a unique combination of advantages that position it to lead a coalition of technologically sovereign middle powers. This document outlines the strategic rationale, the required investments, and the coalition architecture necessary to realize this vision.

## The Convergence Thesis

Three trends are converging to create an unprecedented strategic opportunity for Canada.

**Energy abundance meets compute demand.** Canada's hydroelectric capacity, particularly in Québec, represents a massive competitive advantage as global AI infrastructure becomes increasingly energy-constrained.

**Geopolitical realignment creates space.** The bifurcation of the technology landscape between US and Chinese ecosystems opens a market for sovereign alternatives that middle powers can collectively provision.

**Cryptographic transition demands action.** The approaching reality of quantum computing necessitates a national transition to post-quantum cryptographic standards — a transition that Canada, with its quantum research ecosystem, is well-positioned to lead.

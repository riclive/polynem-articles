The prevailing narrative around sovereign AI infrastructure positions it as a fortress — a walled garden for government data, protected from foreign access. This framing, while politically intuitive, is analytically incomplete.

## The 60% Problem

Our analysis of a Government of Canada AI MVP system reveals a striking finding: approximately 60% of the data required to answer ministerial questions originates from external sources — news, international reports, academic research, and allied government publications.

This means the value proposition of sovereign infrastructure is not primarily about protecting internal data. It is about creating a trusted environment where external and internal data can be safely combined, processed, and analyzed.

## Reframing the Architecture

This finding has profound implications for how Canada should design its sovereign AI infrastructure. The sovereign cloud is not a vault. It is a bridge — a secure intermediary that enables the fusion of external intelligence with internal knowledge while maintaining Canadian jurisdictional control over the processing environment.

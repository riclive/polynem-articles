At Davos, Prime Minister Carney invoked the language of rupture. Not evolution, not transition — rupture. The word choice matters. It signals recognition that the current trajectory of Canada's digital dependency is not a problem to be managed. It is a condition to be broken.

## The Geometry of Sovereignty

The phrase "variable geometry" entered the diplomatic lexicon to describe flexible, issue-specific coalitions that form and reform based on shared interests rather than permanent alliance structures. It is precisely this model that digital sovereignty demands.

No single middle power can build the full stack of digital sovereignty — from semiconductor fabrication to AI model training to post-quantum cryptographic standards. But a coalition of middle powers, each contributing comparative advantage, can collectively achieve what none could alone.

## From Complicity to Agency

Havel's greengrocer did not rebel by tearing down the system. He rebelled by refusing to display the sign. The first act of digital sovereignty is similarly modest: it is the decision to stop defaulting to foreign infrastructure when domestic alternatives exist or could be built.

This is not anti-American or anti-Western. It is pro-Canadian. It is the recognition that allies can be partners without being landlords.
